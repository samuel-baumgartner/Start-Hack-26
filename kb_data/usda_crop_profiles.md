# USDA Crop Profiles for FLORA Greenhouse (Non-Syngenta KB Crops)

These profiles cover the 11 crops NOT in the Syngenta MCP Knowledge Base.
Sources: USDA GRIN database, NASA Biomass Production Chamber (BPC) crop trials, Kennedy Space Center CEA data.

---

## Kale (Brassica oleracea var. sabellica)

**Why Mars-suitable:** Extremely nutrient-dense per m², cold-tolerant, continuous harvest via cut-and-come-again. Top source of Vitamin K, Vitamin C, and calcium — all critical for bone density in low gravity.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 15-21°C (tolerates down to 4°C briefly) |
| PAR | 200-400 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 14-16 h (day-neutral, flexible) |
| pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Water Use | 2.5-3.5 L/m²/day |
| Days to First Harvest | 55-65 (then continuous) |
| Yield | 3.5-5.0 kg/m²/cycle |
| CO₂ Response | Moderate benefit at 800-1200 ppm |

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Seedling (0-14 days):** Temp 18-21°C, light 150 µmol, keep moist. Most vulnerable to damping off.
2. **Vegetative (14-45 days):** Temp 15-20°C, increase PAR to 300+. Rapid leaf expansion. Begin cut-and-come-again at day 35.
3. **Mature/Continuous (45+ days):** Harvest outer leaves, inner leaves continue growing. Can sustain 4-6 months of continuous harvest before quality declines.

## Spinach (Spinacia oleracea)

**Why Mars-suitable:** Fast-growing, high folate and iron content. Supplements packaged food nutrients that degrade in storage.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 15-20°C (bolts above 25°C) |
| PAR | 200-400 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 10-12 h (CRITICAL: long days >14h trigger bolting) |
| pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Water Use | 2.0-3.0 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 35-45 |
| Yield | 2.5-4.0 kg/m²/cycle |
| CO₂ Response | Strong benefit at 800-1200 ppm, 20-30% yield increase |

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Seedling (0-10 days):** Temp 18-20°C, light 150-200 µmol. Sensitive to overwatering.
2. **Vegetative (10-30 days):** Temp 15-18°C optimal. PAR 300-400. Keep photoperiod SHORT to prevent bolting.
3. **Harvest (30-45 days):** Cut outer leaves for continuous harvest, or full plant harvest. Bolting = bitter, reduced nutrition.

**Photoperiod Sensitivity:** Spinach is a long-day plant for flowering. In a greenhouse, KEEP photoperiod at 10-12h to maintain vegetative growth. Exceeding 14h photoperiod will trigger bolting within 1-2 weeks.

## Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum) — Standard

**Why Mars-suitable:** High Vitamin C, lycopene (antioxidant critical for radiation protection), morale crop.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 21-27°C day / 15-18°C night |
| PAR | 400-600 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 14-18 h |
| pH | 5.8-6.5 |
| Water Use | 3.0-5.0 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 70-90 |
| Yield | 8-15 kg/m²/cycle |
| CO₂ Response | Strong benefit, 25-40% yield increase at 1000 ppm |

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Seedling (0-21 days):** Temp 24-27°C, light 200-300 µmol. Stem strength critical.
2. **Vegetative (21-45 days):** PAR 400+. Train to trellis. Prune suckers for determinate varieties.
3. **Flowering (45-60 days):** Needs vibration or manual pollination (no insects on Mars). Temp 21-25°C critical — above 32°C pollen becomes sterile.
4. **Fruiting (60-80 days):** High water demand. Calcium critical to prevent blossom end rot.
5. **Harvest (80-90+ days):** Pick at breaker stage for longest shelf life.

## Cherry Tomato (Solanum lycopersicum var. cerasiforme) — cv. Red Robin

**Why Mars-suitable:** Red Robin is a NASA-selected dwarf determinate cultivar. Compact (30 cm), no staking needed, rapid fruit set. Bred for space constraints.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 20-26°C day / 15-18°C night |
| PAR | 300-500 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 14-16 h |
| pH | 5.8-6.5 |
| Water Use | 2.5-4.0 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 55-70 (faster than standard tomato) |
| Yield | 3-6 kg/m²/cycle |
| CO₂ Response | Strong benefit at 800-1200 ppm |

**Cultivar Notes (Red Robin):** Determinate growth habit — sets fruit without pruning. Max height 30 cm. Self-pollinating but benefits from vibration. Higher fruit count per plant than standard varieties in equivalent space. NASA BPC trials showed reliable fruit set under controlled environment conditions.

**Growth Stages:** Similar to standard tomato but compressed timeline. Flowering begins at day 30-35. Fruit set by day 40-45. First harvest day 55-65.

## Pepper (Capsicum annuum)

**Why Mars-suitable:** Vitamin C density rivals citrus. Capsaicin varieties add flavor diversity to packaged food (crew morale).

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 21-29°C day / 15-18°C night |
| PAR | 300-500 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 14-16 h |
| pH | 5.8-6.5 |
| Water Use | 2.5-4.0 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 60-90 (green) / 80-120 (ripe color) |
| Yield | 4-8 kg/m²/cycle |
| CO₂ Response | Moderate benefit at 800-1000 ppm |

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Seedling (0-21 days):** Temp 25-29°C, slow germinator. Light 200 µmol.
2. **Vegetative (21-50 days):** Temp 22-27°C. Branching architecture, may need support.
3. **Flowering (50-70 days):** Pollination required. Temp above 32°C causes flower drop.
4. **Fruiting (70-90+ days):** Green peppers harvestable earlier. Full color = higher vitamin C.

## Soybean (Glycine max)

**Why Mars-suitable:** Complete protein source (all essential amino acids). High fat content for caloric density. Folate, iron, calcium. Nitrogen-fixing (reduces fertilizer needs).

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 25-30°C day / 18-22°C night |
| PAR | 400-600 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 12-14 h (CRITICAL: short-day plant for flowering) |
| pH | 6.0-6.8 |
| Water Use | 3.0-5.0 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 85-120 (dry seed) |
| Yield | 0.3-0.5 kg/m² dry seed; 2.0-3.5 kg/m² edamame |
| CO₂ Response | Strong benefit, 30-40% biomass increase at 1000 ppm |

**Photoperiod Sensitivity:** Soybean is a short-day plant. Flowering requires photoperiod BELOW 14h. At 16h photoperiod, plants remain vegetative indefinitely. For the greenhouse, maintain 12-13h light during reproductive phase. Can use longer photoperiod during vegetative growth to build biomass, then switch to short days to trigger flowering.

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Seedling/Emergence (0-14 days):** Temp 25-28°C. Rapid root development.
2. **Vegetative (14-45 days):** Can use 16h photoperiod to maximize biomass.
3. **Flowering (45-65 days):** Switch to 12h photoperiod. Temp 25-28°C. Water demand peaks.
4. **Pod Fill (65-95 days):** Critical water period. Edamame harvest at day 75-85 (R6 stage).
5. **Maturity (95-120 days):** Dry down for seed harvest. Full protein + fat content.

## Sweet Potato (Ipomoea batatas)

**Why Mars-suitable:** Highest caloric yield per m² of any crop in the greenhouse. Rich in Vitamin A (beta-carotene), which degrades in packaged food. Edible leaves add greens. NASA BPC staple crop.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 24-30°C (growth stops below 15°C) |
| PAR | 300-500 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 12-14 h (short days promote tuber formation) |
| pH | 5.8-6.5 |
| Water Use | 3.0-4.5 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 90-150 |
| Yield | 5-10 kg/m² tubers + 2-3 kg/m² edible leaves |
| CO₂ Response | Strong benefit, 25-35% tuber yield increase at 1000 ppm |

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Establishment (0-21 days):** Slip planting in soil. Temp 25-30°C. Keep soil moist.
2. **Vine Growth (21-60 days):** Rapid above-ground growth. Train vines. Harvest leaves periodically for greens.
3. **Tuber Initiation (60-90 days):** Short photoperiod (12h) promotes tuberization. Reduce nitrogen.
4. **Tuber Bulking (90-130 days):** Main yield period. Consistent moisture critical.
5. **Harvest (130-150 days):** Cure tubers at 29-32°C for 4-7 days for storage.

## Wheat (Triticum aestivum) — Standard

**Why Mars-suitable:** Caloric staple. Bread wheat provides complex carbs, protein (gluten), B vitamins. Foundation crop for crew diet.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 18-24°C (vernalization NOT required for spring types) |
| PAR | 400-600 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 16-20 h (long-day plant, accelerates flowering) |
| pH | 6.0-7.0 |
| Water Use | 2.5-4.0 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 75-100 |
| Yield | 0.5-1.0 kg/m² grain |
| CO₂ Response | Moderate, 10-20% yield increase at 1000 ppm |

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Seedling/Tillering (0-21 days):** Temp 18-22°C. Dense planting (800-1200 plants/m²).
2. **Stem Extension (21-45 days):** Photoperiod 18-20h accelerates development. PAR 500+.
3. **Heading/Flowering (45-65 days):** Pollination (self-pollinating, wind helps). Temp 18-22°C critical.
4. **Grain Fill (65-85 days):** Water demand peaks. Temp above 30°C reduces grain weight.
5. **Maturity (85-100 days):** Dry down to 12-14% moisture for harvest.

## Dwarf Wheat (Triticum aestivum cv. Apogee)

**Why Mars-suitable:** Apogee is THE NASA-bred space wheat cultivar. Semi-dwarf (40 cm), day-neutral response allows continuous production. Developed at Utah State University specifically for CEA/space applications. Fastest wheat: 60-70 day seed-to-seed cycle.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 22-24°C (tolerates 18-28°C) |
| PAR | 400-800 µmol/m²/s (higher PAR = higher yield) |
| Photoperiod | 20-24 h (day-neutral — continuous light OK) |
| pH | 6.0-6.8 |
| Water Use | 3.0-4.5 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 60-70 (fastest wheat variety) |
| Yield | 0.7-1.2 kg/m² grain (higher than standard wheat in CEA) |
| CO₂ Response | Strong: 30-40% yield increase at 1200 ppm. Apogee bred for high-CO₂ response. |

**Cultivar Notes (Apogee):** Bred by Dr. Bruce Bugbee at Utah State. Key traits:
- Day-length insensitive — no photoperiod manipulation needed
- Semi-dwarf (40 cm) — no lodging risk, space-efficient
- Fast cycle (62-65 days typical) allows 5-6 harvests per year
- Responds exceptionally well to elevated CO₂ (Mars advantage: free atmospheric CO₂)
- Tested extensively in NASA BPC at Kennedy Space Center
- Dense planting at 1000-1500 plants/m² for maximum grain yield

**Growth Stages:** Compressed timeline vs standard wheat.
1. **Seedling/Tillering (0-15 days):** Rapid emergence. 24h light OK.
2. **Stem Extension (15-30 days):** Short internodes (dwarf trait).
3. **Heading (30-40 days):** Self-pollinating.
4. **Grain Fill (40-55 days):** Critical water + temp period.
5. **Maturity (55-70 days):** Harvest at 12% moisture.

## Microgreens (Mixed species)

**Why Mars-suitable:** Harvestable in 7-14 days. 4-40x nutrient concentration vs mature plants. Emergency nutrition source. Minimal resource investment per harvest cycle.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 18-24°C |
| PAR | 100-300 µmol/m²/s (lower light OK) |
| Photoperiod | 12-16 h |
| pH | 5.5-6.5 |
| Water Use | 1.0-2.0 L/m²/day (low) |
| Days to Harvest | 7-14 |
| Yield | 1.5-3.0 kg/m²/cycle (25-40 cycles/year possible) |
| CO₂ Response | Moderate benefit |

**Species Mix for Space:** Radish (7 days, vitamin C), sunflower (10 days, protein), pea shoots (12 days, protein + C), broccoli (10 days, sulforaphane), kale (12 days, calcium + K). Rotate species for nutritional diversity.

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Soak/Germination (0-3 days):** Dark period, mist irrigation. Temp 20-22°C.
2. **Greening (3-7 days):** Expose to light. Rapid chlorophyll development.
3. **Harvest (7-14 days):** Cut at cotyledon or first true leaf stage. Immediate consumption (no storage).

## Dry Beans (Phaseolus vulgaris)

**Why Mars-suitable:** Protein + complex carbs + iron + folate. Storable as dry seed (unlike most fresh vegetables). Nitrogen-fixing. Bush varieties are compact.

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Optimal Temp | 21-27°C (sensitive to cold below 15°C) |
| PAR | 300-500 µmol/m²/s |
| Photoperiod | 12-14 h (day-neutral for most bush types) |
| pH | 6.0-6.8 |
| Water Use | 2.5-4.0 L/m²/day |
| Days to Harvest | 80-100 (dry) / 55-65 (green/snap) |
| Yield | 0.2-0.4 kg/m² dry seed; 2-4 kg/m² green beans |
| CO₂ Response | Moderate, 15-25% increase at 1000 ppm |

**Growth Stages:**
1. **Seedling (0-14 days):** Temp 24-27°C. Direct sow.
2. **Vegetative (14-35 days):** Bush types need no support. Compact canopy.
3. **Flowering (35-50 days):** Self-pollinating. Temp above 32°C causes flower drop.
4. **Pod Fill (50-75 days):** Green bean harvest possible at day 55-65.
5. **Dry Down (75-100 days):** Reduce irrigation. Harvest at 14% moisture.
