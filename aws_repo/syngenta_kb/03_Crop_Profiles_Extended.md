# 3. Extended Crop Profiles for Martian Controlled Environment Agriculture
## 3.1 Purpose
This document provides structured agronomic profiles of selected crops suitable for a Martian greenhouse supporting a crew of four astronauts during a 450-day surface mission.
Each crop profile includes:
* Growth characteristics
* Yield potential
* Resource requirements
* Nutritional contribution
* Environmental sensitivities
* Suitability for mission objectives
The goal is to support AI agents in:
* Crop selection optimization
* Resource allocation decisions
* Nutritional balancing
* Stress prioritization
---
# 3.2 Crop Selection Criteria
Crops were selected based on:
* High edible biomass fraction (harvest index)
* Short to moderate growth cycle
* High nutrient density
* Compatibility with hydroponic systems
* Controlled environment adaptability
Primary crop categories:
* Leafy Greens
* Root/Tuber Crops
* Legumes
* Culinary Herbs
---
# 3.3 Leafy Greens – Lettuce (Lactuca sativa)
## Growth Characteristics
* Growth cycle: 30–45 days
* Harvest index: ~0.7–0.9 (most biomass edible)
* Typical yield (CEA systems): 3–5 kg/m² per cycle
* Root depth: shallow
* Growth habit: vegetative leaf biomass
Fast turnover makes lettuce suitable for continuous harvest systems.
---
## Environmental Requirements
* Optimal temperature: 15–22°C
* Heat stress threshold: >25°C (risk of bolting)
* Optimal relative humidity: 50–70%
* Light requirement: 150–250 µmol/m²/s PAR
* CO₂ enrichment benefit: strong
---
## Water & Nutrient Requirements
* High water demand (high tissue water content ~95%)
* Nitrogen-sensitive crop
* Requires balanced N-P-K solution
* Optimal nutrient solution pH: 5.5–6.5
Water use efficiency improves significantly under hydroponics.
---
## Nutritional Contribution (per 100g fresh weight)
* ~15 kcal
* Rich in Vitamin K
* Good source of Vitamin A (beta-carotene)
* Moderate folate content
* Low protein (~1.4 g)
Role in mission:
Micronutrient density and dietary diversity.
---
## Sensitivity Profile
High sensitivity:
* Heat stress
* Water deficit
* Nitrogen deficiency
Moderate sensitivity:
* Salinity
Low tolerance to environmental fluctuation.
---
## Mission Suitability
Advantages:
* Rapid harvest cycles
* High edible biomass fraction
* Micronutrient rich
Limitations:
* Low caloric density
* Low protein contribution
Best used as:
Micronutrient stabilizer crop in multi-crop system.
---
# 3.4 Potatoes (Solanum tuberosum)
## Growth Characteristics
* Growth cycle: 70–120 days
* Harvest index: ~0.75
* Typical CEA yield: 4–8 kg/m² per cycle
* Growth habit: underground tuber development
High caloric return per unit area.
---
## Environmental Requirements
* Optimal temperature: 16–20°C
* Heat stress threshold: >25–28°C
* Light requirement: 200–400 µmol/m²/s
* Requires good root-zone aeration
---
## Water & Nutrient Requirements
* Moderate to high water requirement
* High potassium demand
* Moderate nitrogen demand
* Sensitive to waterlogging
Optimal nutrient pH: 5.5–6.0
---
## Nutritional Contribution (per 100g fresh weight)
* ~77 kcal
* ~17 g carbohydrates
* ~2 g protein
* Good potassium source
* Low fat content
Role in mission:
Primary carbohydrate and caloric backbone.
---
## Sensitivity Profile
Moderate sensitivity:
* Drought
* Heat stress
High sensitivity:
* Root hypoxia
---
## Mission Suitability
Advantages:
* High calorie density
* High yield per m²
* Relatively stable storage potential
Limitations:
* Longer growth cycle
* Less micronutrient diversity
Best used as:
Primary energy security crop.
---
# 3.5 Radishes (Raphanus sativus)
## Growth Characteristics
* Growth cycle: 21–30 days
* Harvest index: ~0.6–0.8
* Yield: 2–4 kg/m² per cycle
* Compact root vegetable
Very fast biomass turnover.
---
## Environmental Requirements
* Optimal temperature: 15–22°C
* Sensitive to inconsistent watering
* Light requirement: moderate
---
## Water & Nutrient Requirements
* Consistent moisture required
* Moderate nitrogen demand
---
## Nutritional Contribution (per 100g fresh weight)
* ~16 kcal
* Vitamin C source
* Moderate fiber content
Role in mission:
Diet diversity and fast yield buffering.
---
## Sensitivity Profile
High sensitivity:
* Water inconsistency
Moderate sensitivity:
* Heat stress
---
## Mission Suitability
Advantages:
* Very short cycle
* Rapid system feedback crop
Limitations:
* Low caloric density
Best used as:
Short-cycle stability and variety crop.
---
# 3.6 Beans & Peas (Legumes)
Representative crops:
* Common bean (Phaseolus vulgaris)
* Pea (Pisum sativum)
---
## Growth Characteristics
* Growth cycle: 50–70 days
* Harvest index: ~0.5–0.6
* Yield: 2–4 kg/m²
Moderate cycle duration.
---
## Environmental Requirements
* Optimal temperature: 18–25°C
* Sensitive to frost
* Moderate light requirement
---
## Water & Nutrient Requirements
* Moderate water need
* Nitrogen fixation capability (symbiotic bacteria)
* Phosphorus-sensitive
In hydroponics, nitrogen fixation may be reduced and external supply required.
---
## Nutritional Contribution (per 100g fresh weight)
* ~80–120 kcal
* 5–9 g protein
* Complex carbohydrates
* Fiber-rich
Role in mission:
Primary plant-based protein source.
---
## Sensitivity Profile
Moderate sensitivity:
* Salinity
* Water stress
---
## Mission Suitability
Advantages:
* Protein supply
* Nutritional diversity
Limitations:
* Moderate yield compared to potatoes
* Longer growth than leafy greens
Best used as:
Protein security crop.
---
# 3.7 Culinary Herbs (e.g., Basil, Chives)

* Low space requirement

---
## Nutritional & Psychological Role

---

Psychological well-being enhancer.

# 3.9 Strategic Crop Portfolio Logic
Balanced mission crop portfolio should:
* Ensure caloric security (potatoes)
* Ensure protein adequacy (legumes)
* Ensure micronutrient sufficiency (leafy greens)
* Ensure psychological diversity (herbs)
* Maintain short-cycle crop for system resilience (radish/lettuce)
AI agents may dynamically adjust planting density and crop ratios based on:
* Resource availability
* Nutritional deficits
* Environmental instability
* Energy constraints
