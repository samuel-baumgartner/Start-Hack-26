# Syngenta Case Development Framework

## 1. Topic of the Case
**Agriculture on Mars**

## 2. Title of the Case
**Agriculture’s Next Frontier: Feeding Humans on the Red Planet**

## 3. Case Summary

### A. What is the current problem?
NASA is targeting the late 2030s to send astronauts to Mars, a trip that could take up to 9 months each way. Figuring out how to grow nutritionally balanced food on the Red Planet is a critical mission and may also help us grow food in increasingly extreme conditions on Earth.

### B. What is the expected final product?
Hackers will design an autonomous AI agent system to manage a Martian greenhouse. The solution should consist of a working simulation or digital twin of the Martian greenhouse demonstrating their AI agent in action.

### C. Who are the users of this solution?
- NASA planners
- Syngenta scientists in other less extreme settings
- Potentially teams designing autonomous cropping systems for use on Earth

## 4. Data

### What numbers, text, or images are necessary to solve the case?
A knowledge base will be provided, including the following datasets:

1. **Mars environmental data**
   - gravity
   - daylight hours / sunlight intensity
   - atmospheric conditions
   - soil properties
   - other relevant environmental variables

2. **Crop data**
   - yield
   - growth / harvest cycle
   - nutrition requirements
   - water requirements
   - optimum environmental conditions
   - response to abiotic stressors

3. **Human needs**
   - nutritional requirements to support 4 astronauts

Participants are welcome to use other publicly available datasets as well.

## 5. Technology

### Which APIs, SDKs, software, or hardware components are vital to get the job done?
Syngenta will provide an **AWS AgentCore gateway** for hackers to interact with, based on the Syngenta knowledge base.

## 6. Use Case (and Business Case)

### What job does it do well for the users?
Hackers will design an autonomous AI agent system to manage a Martian greenhouse to supplement the diet of a crew of 4 astronauts during a **450-day surface-stay mission on Mars**.

The aim is to:
- maximize nutrient output,
- maintain dietary balance,
- minimize resource consumption.

### Are the participants supposed to come with a use case?
No. The use case is provided.

## 7. Judgment Criteria

### Creativity (25%)
- Originality of the concept
- Innovative problem-solving approach

### Functional / Accuracy / Applicability (25%)
- Scientific accuracy
- Practical usefulness
- Quality of recommendations and outputs

### Visual Design / Accessibility / Ease of Use (25%)
- Clear and attractive interface
- Accessibility
- Ease of understanding and interaction

### Presentation / Demo Quality (25%)
- Clarity of the pitch
- Quality of the demo
- Ability to communicate value and feasibility

## 8. Presentation Prototype

### Format
- A **3-minute PowerPoint presentation**
  - concise pitch of the solution
  - architecture of the AI agent system
  - key features
  - why the approach is innovative and feasible
- A **working simulation or digital twin** of the Martian greenhouse demonstrating the AI agent in action

### Key Elements
- Usage of the provided **AWS AgentCore** system
- Novelty and attractive visual design
- Scientifically accurate results and recommendations

### Requirements
- There must be a **working agent system** behind the PoC / simulation
